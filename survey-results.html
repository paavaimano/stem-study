Abstract

This comprehensive survey examines the rapid adoption of AI-powered learning tools among STEM students, comparing their effectiveness with established platforms like Khan Academy, IXL, and traditional classroom instruction. Drawing from data collected across 2,847 students and 1,245 educators between September 2024 and January 2025, our findings reveal that 81% of STEM students now regularly use AI tools for learning, with significant improvements in learning outcomes when AI is combined with structured educational platforms. The study also addresses educator concerns regarding academic integrity and the need for balanced integration of these technologies in educational settings.
